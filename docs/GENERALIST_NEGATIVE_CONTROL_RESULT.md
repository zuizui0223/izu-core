## Ajania result

All 10 retained *Ajania pacifica* cards had the predeclared ordinal
visible-signal score `3` (highly conspicuous head display). Mean score was 3.0
in each regime. Thus the observed contrasts are:

```text
large Bombus → B. ardens:  0.0
B. ardens → no Bombus:     0.0
```

This is compatible with the negative-control prediction that an open-generalist
flower does not show the specialist-like second visual-signal threshold. It is
not evidence that no generalist trait changes occur: there is only one usable
lineage, the retained cards hit the scale ceiling, and this score measures
visible display only.

## Farfugium attrition

Six *Farfugium japonicum* cards survived stage-0 review, but none belonged to
the Oshima proxy bin. It cannot provide the required two-transition contrast
and is excluded instead of being converted into a two-bin comparison.

## Boundary

These are C-rank photo-derived ordinal data. They contain no direct information
on pollinator identity, pollinator effectiveness, realised outcrossing, or
historical causality. The generalist result is a limited negative control, not
an independent positive replication of the Campanula mechanism.
