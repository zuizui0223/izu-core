# Lilium source-native assessment

## Source

Nakajima R. et al. (2018), *Comparison of floral traits and pollinator assemblages of insular and mainland varieties of Lilium auratum*, Plant Species Biology 33: 276-288. DOI: `10.1111/1442-1984.12222`.

The full text was inspected page by page. The PDF itself is not stored in this repository; only a public-safe extraction with page and figure/table locators is retained.

## What the paper actually compares

The island observations are **Lilium auratum var. platyphyllum** on Izu-Oshima and Aogashima. The mainland observations are **L. auratum var. auratum** in Hachioji and Ranzan. Variety identity is therefore inseparable from island-mainland geography.

The focal mechanism is also not Bombus replacement. The paper tests shifts among Lepidopteran pollinators and floral schedules:

- island var. *platyphyllum*: stronger dependence on crepuscular and nocturnal hawkmoths;
- mainland var. *auratum*: use of both diurnal swallowtail butterflies and nocturnal large hawkmoths;
- associated source-native channels: floral-scent timing, nectar-secretion timing, visit timing and visitor-exclusion reproductive success.

## Extracted findings

1. Both varieties were interpreted as self-incompatible: fruit and seed set after self-pollination were lower than after hand outcrossing in all examined populations (Fig. 4, pp. 280-281).
2. Open-pollinated seed set was lower than hand-outcrossed seed set in several site-years, consistent with pollen limitation, although the site-year significance pattern was heterogeneous (Fig. 5, pp. 280-281).
3. Island-variety floral scent rose in the evening and at night, often with two peaks. Mainland-variety scent was low during the day and highest at night (Fig. 6, p. 281).
4. The Izu-Oshima island sample showed a marked evening/night nectar schedule. Its reported maximum nectar volume was 85.38 uL at 23:00 on day 1; the Ranzan mainland sample had a reported maximum of 44.69 uL at 23:00 on day 2 (Table 1 and Fig. 7, pp. 282-284). These maxima are descriptive, not a formal effect size.
5. Lepidoptera visits on Izu-Oshima were almost entirely nocturnal: 62/62 for spotted flowers and 80/82 for unspotted flowers. Aogashima was mixed day/night (66/151 nocturnal). The authors identify diurnal swallowtails plus nocturnal large hawkmoths as important on the mainland (Table 2, Fig. 9 and Discussion, pp. 283-287).
6. In the Izu-Oshima visitor-exclusion experiment, nocturnally pollinated flowers had higher seed set than diurnally pollinated flowers (`p < 0.01`; Fig. 12, pp. 285-287).

## Eligibility decision

`primary Bombus two-threshold holdout = no`

Reasons:

- different botanical varieties are compared;
- geography and variety identity are confounded;
- the focal effective-pollinator contrast is Lepidoptera, not Bombus;
- the source does not supply the same trait measured across `large_bombus -> ardens -> no_bombus` regimes;
- Oshima and Aogashima differ even within the island variety, so they cannot be collapsed into one simple island state.

## Project role

Retain the source as an **alternative-mechanism anchor**. It demonstrates that island floral differentiation can involve increased temporal specialization of scent and nectar rewards toward a remaining moth guild, rather than the visual-signal decline predicted for loss of an effective Bombus guild.

This is useful in the manuscript as a falsification boundary: the staged Bombus-loss signature is not claimed as a universal island-flower rule. Lilium must remain outside the primary scenario score unless a future analysis explicitly stratifies lineages by pollinator guild and treats inter-varietal comparisons separately.
