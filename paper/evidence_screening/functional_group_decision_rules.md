# Functional-group evidence audit

The first-pass family/genus classifier is a **search prioritisation device**, not a biological observation. Before a species contributes to the U6 mechanism subset, its functional group must be supported by a taxon-specific source.

## Required decision record

For each taxon record:

```text
accepted taxon and taxonomic concept
wild versus cultivated context
geographic scope of pollination evidence
visitor taxa or guilds
visit frequency or qualitative dominance
contact with anthers/stigma, when reported
flower accessibility traits relevant to the grouping
source page/table/figure
assignment: specialist_bee / generalist_open / large_flower / nonbee_special / abiotic_ambig / unresolved
confidence: source-backed_high / source-backed_medium / unresolved
```

## Classification rules

- `specialist_bee`: direct evidence of dependence on large-bodied bees or a long-tongued bee guild, or a floral phenotype plus multiple independent pollination studies supporting restricted access. A tubular corolla alone is insufficient.
- `generalist_open`: direct evidence of a broad visitor spectrum with accessible flowers; family membership alone is insufficient.
- `large_flower`: a source-backed, large-display system where its likely effective vectors are not the lost bumblebee regime. The label is a counter-direction hypothesis, not a guarantee of enlargement.
- `nonbee_special`: source-backed fly, gnat, beetle, moth, bird, or deceptive system that is biologically relevant but outside the primary bumblebee contrast.
- `abiotic_ambig`: wind/water systems, strongly autonomous systems, mixed evidence, or unresolved data. Exclude from the primary moderator test.

## Conflict handling

When the existing family/genus label disagrees with a taxon-specific source, retain both values in the registry. The source-backed assignment replaces the initial label for new analyses, but the initial label remains in the audit trail. No species is reassigned merely because a predicted sign would improve the result.

## High-priority rechecks

1. *Ligustrum ovalifolium* must be reconciled: the current evidence table calls it `intermediate_tubular`, while the family-level classifier puts Oleaceae in `generalist_open`.
2. *Hydrangea macrophylla* and *H. involucrata* require an explicit decision on the functional display trait (fertile flower versus sterile display sepal) before photo or morphology synthesis.
3. *Cirsium nipponicum* and related Asteraceae must be evaluated at taxon level; family-level `generalist_open` and genus-level bee-specialist assumptions are not interchangeable.
4. *Lilium* and *Rhododendron* counter-direction candidates require a source-backed effective-vector assignment before use in the large-flower moderator.
5. *Arisaema*, *Piper*, *Vitis*, and *Cinnamomum* remain specificity controls unless their systems are independently resolved.
